Part 3: Reading & Listening - "No more boring stopovers"

A. Pre-reading Discussion:

  • Read the article below about TripAside.
  • Do you think it's a good idea? Why/Why not?

B. Reading Comprehension - TripAside Article:

No more boring stopovers – go on a guided tour!

Few things are more depressing than spending hours in an airport terminal waiting for a connecting flight. But what if you could turn that dead time into an exciting mini-adventure?

Emmanuel Rozenblum and Anna Veyrenc launched their business in Paris.

There you are, sitting in the departure lounge watching the clock, or ... “And the most frustrating thing, that outside the airport there’s a foreign city which you’d really like to look around, full of great tourist attractions, restaurants, and shops. But you don’t want to leave the airport, because _______, and will miss your flight.”

Frenchman Emmanuel Rozenblum and his sister were on a stopover at Warsaw’s main airport three years ago. But _______, so they decided to go into town.

“We left the airport on our own,” says Mr Rozenblum, “but we didn’t know which bus to take, and _______.”

They caught their flight, and _______, Millions of air travellers make stopovers every year, so Emmanuel thought he could organize short guided trips into the nearest city or countryside.

His idea is that a guide picks up the travellers at the airport, takes them quickly around the sights and to a restaurant, and then _______.

In March 2015, he and business partner Anna Veyrenc started their ‘Stopover Tours’ business, called TripAside, in Paris. _______ and today it has expanded to Frankfurt, London, Brussels, Rome, and Madrid.

Now other small companies are offering similar tours worldwide, from airports kilometres away, so next time you have a long stopover between flights, _______ a book tour!

Adapted from the British press

C. Comprehension Questions:

  1. What is the main problem that TripAside aims to solve?
  2. Who founded TripAside and where did it start?
  3. How long do the tours typically last?
  4. What kind of places do they show people?
  5. Why do Emmanuel and Anna think their idea is a good one?
  6. In which cities did TripAside expand in 2015?
  7. Do they only offer from airports close to the city?

Answer Key (C. Comprehension Questions):

  1. TripAside aims to solve the problem of long, boring stopovers at airports by turning them into short, exciting city tours.
  2. Emmanuel Rozenblum and Anna Veyrenc founded TripAside (originally 'Stopover Tours') in Paris.
  3. The tours typically last a few hours.
  4. They show people great tourist attractions, restaurants, and shops in the city outside the airport.
  5. They think it's a good idea because millions of air travellers have stopovers every year, and it offers a way to experience a foreign city instead of just waiting in the airport.
  6. In 2015, TripAside expanded to Frankfurt, London, Brussels, Rome, and Madrid.
  7. No, the article mentions that other companies (and implicitly TripAside now) offer tours from airports kilometres away, not just those close to the city centre.

Part 4: Grammar Focus

A. Present Simple and Continuous:

Read the article again. Find examples of verbs in the Present Simple and Present Continuous. Discuss their usage.

  • Present Simple examples: (e.g., "Few things are depressing...", "His idea is...", "Emmanuel thinks...")
  • Present Continuous examples: (e.g., "There you are, sitting...", "small companies are offering...")

(Discuss rules: Present Simple for facts, habits, routines. Present Continuous for actions happening now or around now, temporary situations.)

B. Past Simple:

Underline or note down all the verbs in the Past Simple tense from the "TripAside" article.

(e.g., "Emmanuel Rozenblum and Anna Veyrenc launched...", "they decided...", "they caught...")

C. Phrasal Verbs:

Find these phrasal verbs in the text (or think of them in the context of travel) and explain their meaning:

  • "pick up" (as in picking up travellers)
  • "drop off" (as in dropping someone off at the airport)
  • "go into" (as in going into town)

Answer Key (Grammar Focus):

Phrasal Verbs:

  • pick up: to collect someone from a place (e.g., "The guide picks up the travellers at the airport.")
  • drop off: to take someone to a place and leave them there (e.g., "My dad dropped me off at the airport.")
  • go into: to enter a place, often a town or a building (e.g., "They decided to go into town.")
